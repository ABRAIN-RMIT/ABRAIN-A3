Tools

Tools used for the project

Why do we use the tools we do?

All and any project or a goal has needs and requirements. Among those, tools are the most important. We need to know why and what tools to use to perform tasks and meets goals and requirements.

  • MS Teams
  • Monday.com
  • Google Docs
  • Canvas
  • References

Microsoft developed an application called Teams that allows groups and organizations to communicate. Not only does it allow video conferencing, workplace chat, it also integrates with Microsoft's Office 365 and allows for file storage and other organization tools. (Welcome to Microsoft Teams, n.d.) Microsoft Stream is an application that integrates with teams and allows Video on Demand Recordings of any meetings and video conferences you might have had in Microsoft Teams. (Microsoft Stream help & learning, n.d.).
The group mainly used Microsoft Teams and Microsoft Stream to hold and record our meeting sessions. We have found the application to be very useful in this regard as we can always replay important parts of meetings. It was specifically helpful when conducting our interview.

Monday.com is coined as a Work OS (Work Operating System). This specific tool was designed so that Teams and Organisations can confidently assign tasks and manage workflow. This makes collaborating on projects easier and transparent. (What is monday.com?, n.d.) Essentially it is a Team orientated “to do list.”
Monday.com was an excellent tool for the team. It allowed us to efficiently see what tasks needed to be done, and who needed to do them. It allowed us to visualise our goals and made it easy to organise ourselves in order to achieve those goals.

Google Docs is a word processor application created by Google. It is part of their suite of office oriented programs, as such it is very accessible and cloud enabled. It allows for easy collaborations between multiple members of a team, or as individuals. (Google Docs, n.d.)
Our team extensively used Google Docs. We used it for Meeting notes and minutes, drafts for our written content and also allowed us to collaborate on a master document.

Canvas is a web-based learning management system. It is used by learning institutions, educators, and students to access and manage online courses. (What is Canvas?, n.d.) RMIT University is one such learning institution that uses Canvas. As RMIT Students we have decided to maximise the use of Canvas’ tools such as announcements, discussions to keep in touch and make sure everyone is aware of what is happening.
